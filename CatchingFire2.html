CATCHING FIRE-2
×

(watercolour/ year 2015)

With wisdom I had chosen my fire.
I thought I was right, without confusion, Sire!
Till that fateful day.
When the fire burned me, without my choosing.
Scalding pain, smoke in my eyes.
Tears of sorrow and of burning heat.
The suffocation, the laboured breathing.
I remember too well.
I had not chosen those flames.
It takes many scars, many twists and turns,
To finally have the wisdom to choose,
The blessed Fire in which to burn.
But the Irony of Fate plays its music.
Flames blaze high, but not of your choosing.
Just when you put a full-stop, close the door,
Destiny comes by, erases it.
Puts a comma, opens the door.
It makes me wonder...
And question my wisdom,
Is it really possible to choose the fire to burn in?
Perhaps it is an illusion and a foolish desire.
To choose my flame and my fire.
How could Iforget,
Neither is in my power.
